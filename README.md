## 🎯 Business Problem

The organisation was running on a legacy system that had become a liability — unreliable, difficult to maintain, and no longer fit for purpose. But migrating away from it carried significant risk:

- **Business continuity** — operations could not afford downtime during migration
- **Data integrity** — years of critical business data needed to move cleanly
- **Staff dependency** — teams had built their entire workflow around the legacy system
- **No clear migration plan** — the need was known but the path forward wasn't
- **Multiple stakeholders** — IT, operations, finance, and leadership all had different concerns and priorities

Staying on the legacy system was no longer an option — but a poorly managed migration could be worse than staying put.

## 🗂️ Project Phases

### Phase 1 — Discovery & Migration Planning
- Audited the legacy system — data volumes, dependencies, integrations, and processes
- Identified all data entities requiring migration and mapped them to the new system
- Built a phased migration plan with clear milestones, owners, and risk gates
- Defined business continuity protocols for each phase of the migration
- Aligned all stakeholders on the plan, timeline, and their responsibilities

### Phase 2 — Data Audit & Cleansing
- Conducted a full audit of all data in the legacy system
- Identified duplicate, incomplete, and outdated records requiring cleansing
- Coordinated data cleansing exercise across relevant departments
- Established data validation rules to verify integrity post-migration
- Produced a pre-migration data quality report signed off by stakeholders

### Phase 3 — Test Migration
- Executed a full test migration in a sandbox environment
- Validated migrated data against the pre-migration baseline
- Identified and resolved 11 data mapping issues before live migration
- Confirmed all integrations and system dependencies were functioning
- Obtained technical sign-off before proceeding to live migration

### Phase 4 — Cutover Planning & Execution
- Designed a detailed cutover runbook with minute-by-minute task assignments
- Defined rollback criteria and procedures in case of critical failure
- Coordinated a cutover window with minimal business impact
- Executed the live migration with all teams on standby
- Completed post-migration validation — 100% data integrity confirmed

### Phase 5 — Stabilisation & Legacy Decommission
- Provided hypercare support for 4 weeks post-migration
- Resolved all post-migration issues within agreed SLAs
- Confirmed all teams fully operational on the new system
- Decommissioned the legacy system following final sign-off
- Archived all migration documentation for audit purposes

## ⚠️ Risks & Mitigations

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|:---|:---|:---:|:---:|:---|
| 1 | Data loss during migration | Medium | Critical | Full test migration + validation before live cutover |
| 2 | Business disruption during cutover | Medium | Critical | Cutover scheduled in low-traffic window; rollback plan prepared |
| 3 | Data quality issues discovered mid-migration | High | High | Pre-migration data audit and cleansing completed |
| 4 | Staff unable to use new system at go-live | Medium | High | Training completed for 100% of affected staff before cutover |
| 5 | Legacy system dependencies missed | Medium | High | Full system dependency audit completed in Phase 1 |

## 💡 Key Learnings

1. **The cutover runbook is everything** — A migration succeeds or fails at cutover; a minute-by-minute runbook with clear owners and rollback triggers is non-negotiable
2. **Data quality must be fixed before migration, not during** — Attempting to cleanse data mid-migration is the fastest path to delays and errors
3. **Hypercare is an investment, not a cost** — The 4 weeks of post-migration support prevented small issues from becoming business-stopping problems
